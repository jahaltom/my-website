Quantum Random Number Generator

This script generates a true 16-bit random seed by running a quantum circuit on real IBM Quantum hardware. It creates a quantum circuit with 16 qubits, applies Hadamard gates to place them into superposition, and measures the result to extract inherently random bits — a process governed by the laws of quantum mechanics. The resulting binary string is converted into an integer seed, which is then used to initialize NumPy’s random number generator, providing quantum entropy for simulations, machine learning, or cryptographic applications.
